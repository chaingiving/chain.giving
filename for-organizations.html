Overview

Chain.Giving lets any organization or individual launch transparent fundraising campaigns. Our platform manage your operations end-to-end, from collecting donations to distributing tokens redeemable for real-world benefits like vouchers, event tickets, and access passes.

How a Chain.Giving program works

  1. Donors contribute to a program. Each program defines both custom tokens which represent entitlements to goods or services and a fundraising target.
  2. Once the target is reached, the smart contract simultaneously distributes tokens to user wallets and releases the funds to the organization. Every step is recorded on-chain for all to see.
  3. Users redeem their tokens at a participating provider (e.g. a local supermarket, pharmacy, or online store) in exchange for the program's goods or services.
  4. Providers burn redeemed tokens to invoice and receive payments from the organization, closing the loop.

Creating your program

Organizations can run any number of programs. Each program has two parts: a crowdfunding campaign that collects donations, and a token distribution plan that defines what your program users will receive once funding goals are met. Every program is backed by its own smart contract — owned exclusively by your organization — that automatically enforces the rules you set.

Defining your crowdfunding target and deadline

To start receiving donations for a program, set a funding target and a deadline (the number of days the campaign will stay open). On Chain.Giving, deadlines are final and cannot be extended. This guarantees donors a clear, trustworthy timeline. If you need to continue fundraising, simply create a new program.

Define your token types

Each program uses a Multi Token contract (ERC-1155), which lets you create multiple token types within a single program. Tokens can represent anything you need: proof of participation, vouchers for goods or services, unique NFT rewards, and more.

Define your token distribution

Specify who receives each token type and in what quantity. Once fundraising is complete and the program executes, tokens are distributed automatically to the listed beneficiaries. You can also lock a distribution so donors know, before they give, precisely where their contribution will go.

Track token allocation and usage

Beneficiaries can redeem tokens directly through the Chain.Giving wallet built into the app. Every redemption is recorded on-chain, giving your organization and your donors full visibility into how deliverables are used.

Frequently asked questions

Who can join Chain.Giving as an organization?
Registered nonprofits, cultural or charitable associations, as well as individual artists and creators are welcome. We review each applicant to ensure the platform is used for legitimate social good.
How long does onboarding take?
Most organizations complete onboarding within a few days. Contact us to start the process.
Can we convert donations to local currency?
Yes, you can use off-ramp providers (WIP) to convert to local currency where regulation permits.
What does it cost?
Chain.Giving does not charge any platform fees so 100% of each donation goes to its intended purpose. However, blockchains require small gas fees to process transactions (typically less than 1 cent per transaction). Chain.Giving includes a sponsoring system so organizations can cover these gas fees on behalf of their donors. This way, the only cost you pay is the minimal fee that keeps the network secure. No intermediaries take a cut.
How do we prove impact to our donors?
Every token distribution is recorded on-chain, so donors can trace funds from contribution to delivery in real time. You can also export verifiable reports for grant makers and auditors.

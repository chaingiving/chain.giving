FAQ

What is Chain.Giving?
Chain.Giving is a platform that lets organizations run transparent fundraising programs that record their delivery transparently on the blockchain. Donors can follow their contributions from crowdfunding to real-world impact.
Do I need to understand crypto to use it?
No. Both organizations and donors interact with a familiar web app. The blockchain layer handles settlement and auditability behind the scenes — you don't need to run a node or manage complex wallets to get started.
How can Chain.Giving offer zero-fee fundraising?
Chain.Giving is a nonprofit initiative built entirely in our free time. The backend runs on public blockchain infrastructure and the frontend is served from free static file hosting, so operational costs are near zero. Every fundraising program lives in an autonomous smart contract owned solely by its organization wallet — meaning custody and payment processing require no middleman and no platform cut. The only exception: third-party services that convert between fiat and crypto may charge a small processing fee, none of which goes to Chain.Giving.
Which blockchain does Chain.Giving use?
We build our system as a set of Ethereum smart contracts that can be deployed on any EVM-compatible blockchain. As such, Chain.Giving can be deployed to the majority of existing blockchains that support smart contracts.
How is this different from a traditional donation platform?
Traditional platforms settle funds off-chain and report impact with spreadsheets. Chain.Giving ties donations and deliveries on the same ledger, so transparency isn't a PDF report, it's the system itself.
Is Chain.Giving live today?
We're rolling out in stages. Chain.Giving is still in Beta but pilot programs can already be conducted end to end on our testnet deployment. Subscribe to our blog for launch updates.
How can my organization get onboarded?
Reach out to contact@chain.giving and we'll walk you through onboarding, setup, and digitizing your first program.
